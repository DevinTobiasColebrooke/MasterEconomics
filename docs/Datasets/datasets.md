### **Government Data Catalogs (Data.gov)**
These links point to the official US Government open data catalog, filtered by the specific agency responsible for the data.

*   **[U.S. Department of the Treasury](https://catalog.data.gov/organization/treasury-gov)**
    *   **Label:** Treasury Department Datasets
    *   **Focus:** Federal spending, national debt (Debt to the Penny), interest rate statistics (yield curves), IRS tax statistics, and OFAC sanctions lists.
*   **[Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)](https://catalog.data.gov/organization/cfpb-gov)**
    *   **Label:** CFPB Consumer Finance Datasets
    *   **Focus:** The Consumer Complaint Database, mortgage lending data (HMDA), credit card market trends, and student loan research.
*   **[Federal Trade Commission (FTC)](https://catalog.data.gov/organization/federal-trade-commission)**
    *   **Label:** FTC Consumer Protection & Antitrust Data
    *   **Focus:** Fraud and identity theft reports (Consumer Sentinel Network), merger enforcement actions, and Do Not Call registry statistics.
*   **[Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC)](https://catalog.data.gov/organization/fdic-gov)**
    *   **Label:** FDIC Banking Industry Data
    *   **Focus:** Financial profiles of insured banks, "Call Reports," bank failure history, and deposit market share statistics.
*   **[National Credit Union Administration (NCUA)](https://catalog.data.gov/organization/national-credit-union-administration)**
    *   **Label:** NCUA Credit Union Datasets
    *   **Focus:** Financial performance reports for credit unions, call report data, merger activity, and credit union chartering information.
*   **[U.S. Department of Commerce (DOC)](https://catalog.data.gov/organization/doc-gov)**
    *   **Label:** Department of Commerce Datasets
    *   **Focus:** A broad aggregation of data from major statistical bureaus including the **Census Bureau** (demographics), **Bureau of Economic Analysis** (GDP and personal income), **NOAA** (weather and climate), and the **International Trade Administration** (export/import statistics).
*   **[Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC)](https://catalog.data.gov/organization/commodity-futures-trading-commission)**
    *   **Label:** CFTC Derivatives & Market Reports
    *   **Focus:** The "Commitment of Traders" (COT) reports (open interest breakdown), weekly swaps market data, financial reports for Futures Commission Merchants (FCMs), and bank participation in futures markets.

### **Federal Reserve APIs & Collections**
These links point to specialized tools for developers and researchers to pull economic and market-specific data.

*   **[FRED API (St. Louis Fed)](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/docs/api/fred/overview.html/)**
    *   **Label:** Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) API
    *   **Focus:** A powerful developer tool providing programmatic access to over 800,000 economic time series (e.g., GDP, CPI, unemployment, and interest rates) from various global sources.
    The Federal Reserve's discount window primary credit rate is 3.75% as of January 6, 2026. You can access this data programmatically using the Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) API with the series ID DPCREDIT
*   **[Federal Reserve Board Data (FRED Collection)](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/collection/federal-reserve-board-data)**
    *   **Label:** Board of Governors Statistical Releases
    *   **Focus:** A curated subset of FRED data specifically published by the Federal Reserve Board, including money supply (H.6), consumer credit (G.19), and industrial production (G.17).
*   **[New York Fed Markets API](https://markets.newyorkfed.org/static/docs/markets-api.html)**
    *   **Label:** NY Fed Market Operations API
    *   **Focus:** Real-time and historical data on monetary policy implementation, including the Effective Federal Funds Rate (EFFR), Secured Overnight Financing Rate (SOFR), repo operations, and SOMA (System Open Market Account) holdings.

### **SEC EDGAR api**
Public company data
*   **[U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)](https://www.sec.gov/search-filings/edgar-application-programming-interfaces)**
    *   **Label:** SEC EDGAR APIs
    *   **Focus:** Programmatic access to the massive EDGAR database, allowing developers to query real-time and historical corporate filings (10-Ks, 10-Qs, 8-Ks), extract XBRL financial statement data, and search company submission histories.

### **IRS Applicable Federal Rates**
*   **[IRS Applicable Federal Rates](https://www.irs.gov/applicable-federal-rates)**
    *   **Label:** IRS AFR
    *   **Focus:** The IRS uses the AFR to prevent individuals from using artificially low or zero-interest loans to disguise taxable events (like income or gifts) as non-taxable loans. If a loan's interest rate is set below the corresponding AFR, the IRS may "impute" interest, meaning it will treat the transaction as if the minimum interest was charged and tax the lender accordingly, even if no cash changed hands
