# Macroeconomic Supply

## The Classical Definition
In the classical economic framework defined by Henry Dunning Macleod, **Supply** is not the physical volume of a material that exists in the world. Supply is strictly defined by the act of offering an **Economic Quantity** for sale.

*   **Production:** To "Produce" in economics means to bring forward and offer for sale.
*   **Supply:** The aggregate of all Economic Quantities (Money, Labour, or Credit) that are actively being produced (offered for sale) in the market.
